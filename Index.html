LEADING DRUMMER

STEPHEN CREIGHTON May 2, 2016

Our Leading Drummer is called Stephen Creighton, you won't meet anyone in the pipe band world who has not been dumbfounded by his drumming technique and flawless execution! Stephen has had a lot of success with the band in solo competitions and band competitions. He has won multiple All Ireland solo competitions at Senior Drumming (the highest grade). He won the World drumming solos at Junior level! He now competes in the Adult section where he has made 24 final out of the 27 he has entered! 12 are in the final and he is currently holding the 4th place medal. He strives to make the drum corp the best they can be and demands musical and rythmic perfection! He is sought after to teach many masterclasses and is always a familiar face judging at solo and smaller band competitions.

He currently has merchandise up for sale along with his one line of Snare sticks and his own drum pad. He worked alongside Evans to produce the first black snare head, which was used by the drum corp season along with PSNI also using them!

Pipe Major

ALEN TULLY

Behind every great band there is a great leader. Alen is going into his 6th year as being Pipe Major after his dad gave him the ranks back in 2013, although his dad still plays in the band. He is recognised internationally at solo and band events, at has received great success at solo competitions as well as with his pipe corp. He has competed multiple times at the Metro Cup along with other famous pipers. He has 2 kids and is married to a past tenor drummer of the band, Michelle! The band currently play a tune named after his daughter which was written by his father, it's called "Miss Amelia Tully", who is 6 years old. He has really guided the band up the ranks with being in the top 3 at almost all Majors... including Worlds. This year is the year the band are hosting the Pre Worlds Concert. The last time the band hosted this concert (2010) they won the World Pipe Band Championships for the first time, we can do it again!!

Competitions

We compete all over Ireland and Scotland from May to August every year. On average we have 9/10 competitions a year. We travel to Scotland 4 times a year for Majors ( British, Scottish, Eurpoean, and World Championships), luckily the UK championships are in Stormont in Belfast. Luckily the Ulster, Bangor, Lisburn and All Ireland Champiopnships are only up the north. Travelling is a major part of pipe band, and it can be expensive. Our biggest journey is Forres for the European Championships, we have to travel over 6 hours on a bus after a short one hour flight... at 6 in the morning! We then compete the next day and are well rested. Worlds is by far the biggest competition, with almost 300 bands competing every year. Being in the highest grade ( Grade 1) we have two days of competing and the other grades only compete on the 2nd day. In the qualifiers (1st day) we have to play a medley and MSR and place in the top 6 to qualify for the finals tomorrow- we have qualified every year coming mostly in 1st and 2nd and winning the drumming. The next day up to 30,000 people gather around the huge main arena and watch the grade one finals. We have stayed consistently in the top 3, and the year we did the concert (2010) we won. We are doing the concert again this year so I hope we can do it this year!
